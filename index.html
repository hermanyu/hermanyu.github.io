Here are the 2 equations that made me fall in love with mathematics:

\(\begin{align*} \int_1^{\infty} 2\pi\frac{1}{x} \sqrt{1+\frac{1}{x^4}} dx &= \infty \\ \int_1^{\infty}\pi\frac{1}{x^2}dx &= \pi \end{align*}\)

At first glance, this seems like a sequence of completely unintelligible symbols. But really, what is being communicated here is something simple yet extrordinary. The two equations give the Surface Area and Volume (resp.) of a solid called Gabriel's Horn . The equations are saying: 1) The horn has an infinite surface area and 2) The horn has a finite volume. It seems mind-bending yet mesmorizing to imagine such a geometric object.
